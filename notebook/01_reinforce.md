# Introduction

## REINFORCE

REINFORCE is a simple policy optimization algorithm for reinforcment learning.

Let $\pi_\theta(a\mid s)$ be a Markov *policy* parameterized by $\theta$ that defines the probability of taking action $a$ as a function of state $s$ within the environment.

A *rollout* (or *epsiode* or *trajectory*) $\tau$ consists of a sequence of samples $a_t$, $s_t$, $r_t$. A rollout is generated by taking the environment's initial state $s_0$ and repeatedly sampling $a\sim \pi_\theta$ stochastically until an end condition is reached.

- $r_t$ denotes the *reward*, most commonly as a  function $r(s,a,s')$ of the transition from state $s$ to state $s'$ by taking action $a$.
- The *return* $R(\tau)$ of a rollout $\tau$ is the sum of rewards starting from the initial state, subject to exponential decay:

$$R(\tau)=\sum_{t=0}\gamma^{t} r_t$$

The maximization objective is the expected return over rollouts $\tau$.

$$J(\theta)=\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_\theta}[R(\tau)]$$

The issue is that the distribution generating $\tau$ itself depends on $\theta$, therefore computing $\nabla_\theta J(\theta)$ is intractable.

### The Log-Derivative Trick

For any parameterized density function $p_\theta$,

$$\nabla_\theta p_\theta(x)=p_\theta(x)\nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x)$$

This identity allows you to write the following expectation as follows:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\nabla_\theta\mathbb{E}_{x\sim p_{\theta}}[f(x)] &= \nabla_\theta\int p_\theta(x) f(x)\,dx \\ 
&= \int \nabla_\theta\, p_\theta(x) f(x)\,dx \\ 
&= \int p_\theta(x)\nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x)\,f(x)\, dx \\
&= \mathbb{E}_{x\sim p_\theta}[f(x)\, \nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x)]
\end{aligned}
$$

When applied to rewards and trajectories,

$$\nabla_\theta\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_{\theta}}[R(\tau)] = \mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_{\theta}}\left[R(\tau) \nabla_\theta\sum_{t\in\tau} \log \pi_\theta(a_t\mid s_t)\right]$$

### The Causality Optimization

The action at time $t$ has no bearing on the rewards collected before time $t$. Multiplying the sum over $t$ by past rewards contributes per-timestep variance. $R(\tau)$ in the sum can be replaced by the return-from-time $t$ which we denote

$$G_t=\sum_{k=t}\gamma^{k-t}r_k$$

We can then rewrite our objective gradient as follows:

$$\nabla_\theta\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_{\theta}}[R(\tau)] = \mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_{\theta}}\left[ \nabla_\theta\sum_{t\in\tau} G_t\log \pi_\theta(a_t\mid s_t)\right]$$

### Algorithm

REINFORCE follows the given steps:

- Roll out $N$ trajectories under $\pi_\theta$.
- Compute $G_t$ over all $t$ for all $\tau$. Typically, you would subtract the mean of $G_t$ from $G_t$ to reduce variance.
- Compute $J(\theta)=\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim \pi_{\theta}}[R(\tau)]$.
- Update $\theta \gets \theta + \alpha \nabla_\theta J(\theta)$
